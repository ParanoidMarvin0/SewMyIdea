Beginner – no, all fabric and supplies are provided Intermediate I – students provide fabric, and notions (ex: thread, elastic) Intermediate II – students provide patterns, fabric, and notions
The capacity of the beginner class is 8 students, and the capacity of each intermediate class is 6 students
The beginning of every class starts with a 20 min lesson on a specific topic. Students are provided a workbook of summary notes. After the lesson, students work on their sewing projects and apply the lessons.
The intermediate class suits most students who have some sewing experience. Review the class descriptions to help you decide which class suits your needs. If you would like a refresh on fundamental skills, try the beginner class. If you know how to sew and are getting familiar with patterns, try the intermediate I class. If you have made many garments from patterns and would like to achieve a better fit and finish, try the intermediate II class.
Sewing classes are offered multiple times a year during the fall and winter. No classes are available during the summer from May to September.
The class is designed for adults, and as such, the lessons are too advanced for most children. Furthermore, close supervision is not provided while working with hot irons and sharp objects. Accommodations may be made in special circumstances with instructor permission. References for sewing teachers that offer children’s sewing classes is also available upon request.
